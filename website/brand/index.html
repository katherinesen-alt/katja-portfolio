Color is the
loudest thing I say.

A confident, high-contrast palette built around deep ink and soft off-white, energized by two saturated accents — electric blue and warm pink. Use accents one at a time. Never mixed at full strength.

One accent at a time

Pair a single accent with ink + off-white. Never mix blue and pink at full strength on the same surface.

Off-white is the default

80% of surfaces are off-white. Ink and accents earn their place by carrying the message.

Off-white beats pure white

Pure white is forbidden. It flattens the system. The warm off-white softens type and reduces glare.

Contrast is non-negotiable

Body text is always ink on off-white or ink on accent. No grey-on-grey, no faint accent-on-accent.

A serif and a sans,
kept close.

Two typefaces, one for the loud work and one for the quiet. Playfair Display carries headlines, display, and italic accents — a high-contrast serif with editorial grace. DM Sans carries body, metadata, and UI — geometric, neutral, easy at small sizes.

Direct.
Curious.
Warm.
Crafted.

Four words I write before any work begins. Every paragraph, screen, and email gets measured against them — if a sentence fails one, it fails the brand.

01 / Direct

Say the thing.

Lead with the point. No hedging, no preamble, no “I think maybe”. The reader's time is the budget.

02 / Curious

Ask first.

Questions are the work. Treat “I don't know yet” as a strength. The next sentence is where the value is.

03 / Warm

Talk to a person.

Contractions, second person, the occasional aside. Confident doesn't have to mean cold.

04 / Crafted

Sweat the verbs.

Short clauses. Strong verbs. Cut adjectives. If a sentence can lose a word, it loses the word.

Meet the cat.

A black cat in halftone print, with one bright blue eye and a white spark of fur. She holds whichever pose the page asks for — sitting, mid-jump, head-on stare, profile, mouth open. Pick the cut that fits the moment; the brand stays one cat.
