One accent at a time

Pair a single accent with ink + cream. Multi-accent layouts collapse into the gradient instead.

Cream is the default

80% of surfaces are cream. Ink and accents earn their place by carrying the message.

Cream beats white

Pure white is forbidden. It flattens the whole system. Cream warms type and reduces glare.

Contrast is non-negotiable

Body text must be ink-on-cream or ink-on-accent. No grey-on-grey, no faint accent-on-accent.

The gradient is
the signature.

Three gradients, ranked by weight. Primary is the always-on workhorse, used on every cover, avatar, and accent shape. Secondary and Tertiary step in for variety when you need a second voice on the page.

Voice / four words to live by

Direct.
Curious.
Warm.
Crafted.

01 / Direct

Say the thing.

Lead with the point. No hedging, no preamble, no “I think maybe”. The reader's time is the budget.

02 / Curious

Ask first.

Questions are the work. Treat “I don't know yet” as a strength. The next sentence is where the value is.

03 / Warm

Talk to a person.

Contractions, second person, the occasional aside. Confident doesn't have to mean cold.

04 / Crafted

Sweat the verbs.

Short clauses. Strong verbs. Cut adjectives. If a sentence can lose a word, it loses the word.

Meet the cat.

A black cat in halftone print, with one bright green eye and a white spark of fur. She holds whichever pose the page asks for: sitting, mid-jump, head-on stare, profile, mouth open. Pick the cut that fits the moment; the brand stays one cat.
